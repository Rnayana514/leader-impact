A. A Serious Problem
Rajendra’s life had been characterized by years of immorality and rebellion against God. After receiving Christ, he experienced many highs and lows. He often felt deep guilt as he reflected on his past. Doubts would often multiply and he would wonder if Christ had really come into his life or if he had really been forgiven. Gradually, he stopped all attempts to cultivate his relationship with God. He became convinced that the abundant life in John 10:10 was only meant for special people. He finally lived in fear of what God would do to him if he continued to fall back into doing things he knew were wrong.

* What was this man’s problem? How do you think God felt as He watched this man in his situation?

2. What needs to happen when we realize we have sinned (I John 1:9)?

If we confess our sins, He is faithful and righteous to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us from all unrighteousness. 1 John 1:9
3. The following three elements are essential to the concept of true “confession”:

a Call sin "sin." Recognize it for the disobedience, defiance and rebellion it really is. An example in II Chronicles 29:6
b Call sin "forgiven." Christ's work on the cross is wholly sufficient. Romans 8:1
c Call on God to change you.Repentance is a 180o change in mind - turning from self-effort to trusting God to change you. Note-True repentance may require restitution. - Romans 8:6-8
